# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Readers of financial and technological thrillers, arriving from social posts, press
coverage, book clubs, and paid campaigns. They land curious but unconvinced, usually
on a phone, usually mid-scroll. The job to be done is to decide whether this book is
worth their evening, and then to buy it without friction.

A second, smaller audience is press and book club organizers, who need the cover,
the blurb, and the assets quickly and without a pitch.

## Product Purpose

Sell *The Midnight Coder's Children* ahead of its 22 September 2026 release, through
three routes: pre-orders of the $14.99 digital edition direct from the author, print
sales at Amazon and Barnes & Noble, and email list growth for launch day.

Success is a reader who samples the first chapter and buys, and an author who keeps
the full margin rather than a retailer's share.

## Brand Personality

Tense, precise, technically literate. The voice of someone who has actually worked
inside the systems the book is about, writing at 5:43 in the morning when something
has already gone wrong.

Confident without swagger. The book earns attention through specificity rather than
adjectives. Never breathless, never salesy.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS pricing pages.** No tier cards, feature checklists, or hero metrics.
- **Amazon product pages.** No star ratings, breadcrumbs, or retail chrome. The site
  does not compete with Amazon on Amazon's terms.
- **The faux-code-block treatment** currently on `/buy` and `/buy/success`, copied
  from the 404 page. Decorative rather than communicative, and it sells nothing.
- **Generic dark AI landing pages.** No purple gradients, glassmorphism, or floating
  glow orbs.

## Design Principles

1. **Two voices, one system.** Monospace is the machine speaking: prices, labels,
   status, buttons. Serif is the book speaking: title, blurb, pull quote. Hierarchy
   comes from the contrast between them, not from size alone.
2. **The cover is the argument.** It is the single most persuasive asset available.
   Give it room and light it deliberately rather than shrinking it into a layout.
3. **Atmosphere over ornament.** Tension is built with depth, contrast, and restraint.
   Any effect that does not raise the temperature is removed.
4. **Say the awkward thing plainly.** This is a pre-order for a book that does not
   exist yet. Stating the release date clearly prevents the support email later.
5. **Never let the motif outrank the message.** The terminal conceit serves the sale.
   Where the two conflict, the sale wins.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text at or above 4.5:1 against the midnight background, large text
at or above 3:1. Every animation needs a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative.
Content must never be gated behind a transition, so a reveal that fails to fire
cannot ship a blank page. Interactive targets at least 44px. Full keyboard access
with visible focus states.
